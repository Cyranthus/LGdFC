### Universal Toy Template

Use this same template for all toys.

#### A. Goal and Method

This determines behavior in play.

- What does this toy want?
- How does it normally try to get it?

#### B. Performance Hooks (limit to two)

Choose two cues that make this toy easy to run at the table. The goal is instant recall, not detail.

Examples:

- Physical habit or detail
- Speech pattern or verbal habit
- Emotional baseline
- Repeated behavior

#### C. Active Problem

Define something that is currently going wrong. This problem exists whether the players interact with it or not.

Questions to answer:

- What pressure is the toy under right now?
- What happens if nothing interferes?
- How will this situation change over time?

### Campaign-Level Prep (Toy Chest)

#### Initial Setup

This forms the core of the campaign. Prepare:

- 6–10 NPC toys
- 3–5 faction toys
- 2–3 location toys

#### Weekly Prep

Limit weekly prep to answering:

1. Which toys are most likely to interact with the party?
2. How have their problems changed since last session?
3. Is one new toy needed to respond to player actions?

### Session Use

If unsure what happens next during play:

- Identify the relevant toy
- Refer to its goal
- Apply its method under current pressure

### Practice Exercise

Before a session:

1. Choose one existing NPC or location
2. Rewrite it using the toy template
3. Add a current problem
4. Do not plan outcomes

### Ongoing Use

This guide is meant to be referenced briefly during prep. Over time, most of the information should move from notes to memory. The method improves through repetition rather than expansion.
